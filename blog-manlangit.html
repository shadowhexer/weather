A Constitutional Draft for the New Era

  • Hexer
  • Jan 1, 2024
  • 0 Comments

"The once sovereign kingdoms and states were united by the Spanish explorers in the name of King Felipe II where the name Philippines is derived from."

- Preamble, Plurinational Draft

This is the history of the Philippines. By the time it was known for its name, the islands has been ruled by many independent kingdoms and states. They either fought alongside/against each other, or trade with each other. These sovereign states had their own government, laws, and culture.

By the time the Spanish adventurers came, they turned the sovereign states against each other in order to consolidate power and eventually, ruled the northern island and the center islands for the next 333 years.

During this era, the rule of the colonial government is highly centralised and the power is concentrated in the hands of the colonial Governor-General. The Governor-General was the representative of the Viceroy of New Spain based in Mexico City, which in turn is appointed by the King of Spain, until 1821 when the Mexican Empire gained independence. Since then, until 1898, the Spain directly ruled the Philippines and enacted several reforms, but the highly centralised government still persist.

First Republic

When Spain concede under a mock battle against the Americans, the Revolutionaries declared the independence of the country upon the creation of the Malolos Constitution that features a parliamentary system. While the republic was short lived, the centralised government continued to exist under the Malolos system.

Commonwealth Era and the Third Republic

After the Revolutionaries concede defeat to the Americans, the First Republic was abolished and replaced by a centralised, presidential system with powers centered on the Governor-General and eventually, the High Commissioner. The central government decides on most aspects of the country. This has continued under the 1935 constitution when the Philippines was granted "independence" by the United States to loosen their burden as a result of the World War 2.

The Fourth Republic

When Marcos Sr. took over the post, declared martial law, and lift it, the Fourth Republic was born. While the framers of the 1973 constitution planned for a centralised parliamentary republic similar to that of Germany (then West Germany), the 1981 amendments provided for a presidential system where the Prime Minister was the head of the Cabinet, and the Executive Committee, composed of the Cabinet and 14 other members, acts as the adviser to the President.

During this time, the Prime Minister was the leader of the Government Business to the National Assembly, as opposed to being the leader of the executive branch. The President controls the ministries and has the power to dissolve the Assembly. This effectively legitimised the concentration of power not only to the central government but also to the Presidency.

The Fifth Republic

After the fall of the Marcos regime, the framers of the 1987 constitution sought to return the separation of powers between the executive and the legislative. The 1987 constitution was drafted in the span of four months that contradict provisions was not fixed and even made it harder to amend.

The 1987 constitution, not only it retained the centralised system, but also legitimised personality politics through direct election of almost all offices, weaker checks and balances, the introduction of the party-list system, and the national election of the upper house. The 1987 constituion institutionalised political dynasty by adding term limits, return on separation of powers, and etc. The 1987 constitution also cemented vote buying by the continuation of the nationalist protectionist economy that favoured the oligarchs, which causes fewer jobs, low salary, and surge of OFWs.

What is to be done?

As the product of the 1987 constitution, I experienced the hardships my family went through and still going. As I become aware of the social problems, I found out that the system, branded as the "Freedom Constitution", is not free at all. There is no freedom when you are hungry. This is the reason why I made this constitution; changing the fate of this country and break the shackles of the colonial rule.
